OFFICIAL RULEBOOK

Union Roleplay Rules

These rules protect the quality, fairness and enjoyment of roleplay throughout Union City. By playing on the server, you agree to follow every rule listed on this page.

Read the Rules Ask for Help
⚠️

Before You Begin

Not knowing a rule does not excuse breaking it. Rules may be updated as the community develops, so players are responsible for checking this page regularly.

Staff may take action against behaviour that damages roleplay or the community, even where a specific situation is not described word-for-word in this rulebook.

01
COMMUNITY STANDARDS

Community Rules

Standards that apply across the game server, Discord and all Union Roleplay platforms.

1.1 Respect and Inclusion +

Treat every member of the community with respect. Harassment, bullying, discrimination, hate speech, threats or targeted abuse are prohibited.

This applies whether behaviour occurs in character, out of character, through direct messages or on another platform connected to Union Roleplay.

1.2 No Toxic Behaviour +

Do not deliberately provoke arguments, create unnecessary drama, insult other players or attempt to ruin another person's experience.

Losing an in-game situation is not a reason to become hostile out of character.

1.3 Appropriate Content +

Extremely graphic, sexual, hateful or otherwise inappropriate content is not permitted.

Roleplay involving sensitive subjects must remain reasonable, consensual where appropriate and within the community's standards.

1.4 Advertising and Promotion +

Advertising other FiveM servers, Discord servers, communities, products or services without prior permission is prohibited.

1.5 Account Responsibility +

You are responsible for all activity carried out through your account. Do not share accounts or allow another person to use your identity to access the server.

1.6 Staff Instructions +

Follow reasonable instructions given by authorised staff members. Staff decisions should not be argued during active roleplay or in public channels.

Concerns about a decision should be raised through the correct support or appeal process.

02
ROLEPLAY STANDARDS

General Roleplay Rules

Rules designed to protect believable, story-focused and fair roleplay.

2.1 Remain In Character +

Remain in character while participating in active roleplay. Do not discuss staff reports, rules, Discord conversations or other out-of-character matters during a scene.

Use the appropriate support system after the scene has ended if assistance is required.

2.2 Metagaming +

Metagaming is using information your character did not learn through legitimate in-character means.

Examples:
  • Watching a livestream to locate another player.
  • Using information from Discord during an active scene.
  • Recognising a masked person purely from their player name or voice without proper roleplay.
2.3 Powergaming +

Powergaming means forcing an action or outcome on another player without giving them a reasonable opportunity to respond.

It also includes using unrealistic mechanics, animations or actions to gain an unfair advantage.

2.4 Random Deathmatch +

Random Deathmatch, commonly called RDM, means attacking or killing another player without a valid roleplay reason or meaningful interaction.

Violence must be supported by the events of the scene and should not be used as the first response to every disagreement.

2.5 Vehicle Deathmatch +

Vehicle Deathmatch, commonly called VDM, means deliberately using a vehicle as a weapon without suitable roleplay justification.

Accidents should be roleplayed properly rather than ignored.

2.6 Value of Life and FearRP +

Your character must value their life and react to credible danger. Behave as a reasonable person would when faced with weapons, serious threats or overwhelming force.

Do not ignore a clear threat simply because game mechanics may allow you to escape or fight back.

2.7 Combat Logging +

Do not disconnect to avoid arrest, injury, robbery, consequences or any other active roleplay.

If your game crashes or your connection drops, make a reasonable attempt to return and contact the involved players or staff where necessary.

2.8 Unrealistic Roleplay +

Actions must remain believable within the setting. Avoid impossible stunts, repeatedly ignoring serious injuries, abusing game physics or behaving in ways that exist only to create chaos.

03
CHARACTER STANDARDS

Character and Life Rules

Requirements covering character creation, injuries, death and information.

3.1 Realistic Character Names +

Characters must have believable first and last names. Celebrity names, joke names, offensive names and deliberately unrealistic names are not allowed.

3.2 Character Separation +

Information, relationships and assets belonging to one character must not automatically transfer to another character.

Your characters should have separate stories, knowledge and motivations.

3.3 New Life Rule +

When your character dies and respawns, they must forget the immediate circumstances leading to their death.

You must not return directly to the scene, seek revenge or use knowledge obtained during the event unless staff or an approved medical outcome states otherwise.

3.4 Injury Roleplay +

Roleplay injuries according to their seriousness. Major crashes, shootings, falls and explosions should have meaningful consequences.

Do not immediately return to strenuous activity after receiving treatment for a severe injury.

3.5 Permanent Character Death +

A permanent character death must be chosen by the character's owner unless an approved storyline or staff decision states otherwise.

Other players cannot force you to permanently delete or retire your character.

04
CRIMINAL ROLEPLAY

Crime Rules

Criminal activity must create meaningful roleplay rather than exist only to generate money or conflict.

4.1 Crime Must Create Roleplay +

Criminal activity should create interaction, investigation and consequences. Avoid repeatedly committing the same offence without any wider story or character motivation.

4.2 Robbery Standards +

Give victims a fair chance to understand and respond to demands. Instructions must be clear, realistic and possible to follow.

Do not immediately attack a compliant victim or use robbery mechanics only to empty another player's inventory without meaningful roleplay.

4.3 Hostage Roleplay +

Hostages must be genuine participants in the scene. Using friends, alternate characters or willing associates as fake hostages is prohibited.

Demands must remain reasonable and must allow emergency services a genuine opportunity to negotiate.

4.4 Gang and Group Limits +

Criminal groups must follow any active group-size limits published by Union Roleplay.

Do not bypass limits by splitting into separate groups that continue to cooperate in the same scene.

4.5 No Forced Criminal Roleplay +

Do not force another player to transfer ownership of businesses, vehicles, property or large amounts of money through threats alone.

4.6 Police Baiting +

Do not deliberately commit pointless offences solely to attract police attention, begin a pursuit or provoke armed officers.

05
ROAD SAFETY

Vehicle Rules

Vehicles must be used in a believable way that respects the setting and other players.

5.1 Drive Realistically +

Drive according to road conditions, vehicle type and the seriousness of the situation.

Excessive speeding, driving over mountains, repeated high-speed collisions and impossible jumps are not acceptable outside a properly developed scene.

5.2 Vehicle Damage +

Roleplay significant damage after serious crashes. A vehicle that has suffered severe damage should not continue operating normally.

5.3 Off-Road Driving +

Standard road vehicles should not be driven across steep hills, railway lines, staircases or terrain they could not realistically manage.

5.4 Pursuit Standards +

Pursuits should remain believable and should not become a sequence of unrealistic stunts simply to avoid capture.

Consider surrendering when your vehicle is disabled, surrounded or escape is no longer realistic.

5.5 Emergency Vehicles +

Do not steal emergency-service vehicles without a strong and developed roleplay reason.

Emergency vehicles must not be used for casual transport, trolling or impersonating a department.

06
PUBLIC SERVICES

Emergency Service Rules

Department members are expected to maintain high standards of professionalism and roleplay.

6.1 Professional Standards +

Police, medical and other emergency-service members must follow their department procedures, training and chain of command.

6.2 No Corruption Without Approval +

Corrupt emergency-service roleplay must receive prior approval from senior management.

Misusing department equipment, databases or powers without permission may result in removal from the department and further staff action.

6.3 Department Information +

Confidential information obtained through police, medical or government systems must not be transferred to another character or used for personal advantage.

6.4 Medical Roleplay +

Medical staff should assess injuries, provide appropriate treatment and communicate with patients.

Patients must cooperate with reasonable treatment roleplay and should not instantly ignore serious injuries after being revived.

6.5 Use of Force +

Police use of force must be reasonable, proportionate and justified by the threat presented.

Firearms should not be used where lower levels of force remain safe and practical.

07
ECONOMY AND BUSINESSES

Business Rules

Businesses should contribute to the economy and create opportunities for player interaction.

7.1 Business Activity +

Business owners must make a reasonable effort to keep their business active and accessible to the community.

7.2 Fair Employment +

Owners should treat employees fairly and must not use business permissions to steal employee assets, wages or personal property.

7.3 Economy Exploitation +

Exploiting scripts, duplicating items, transferring money between alternate characters or abusing business systems is prohibited.

Bugs affecting money, items or property must be reported immediately.

7.4 Asset Transfers +

Large transfers of money, vehicles, property or business assets must have a legitimate in-character reason and may be reviewed by staff.

08
PROTECTED AREAS

Safe Zones

Certain areas receive additional protection to prevent disruption and unfair conflict.

8.1 Protected Locations +

Safe zones may include character spawn locations, hospitals, police stations, government buildings and other locations announced by staff.

8.2 Starting Conflict +

Do not begin robberies, kidnappings, assaults or other avoidable criminal activity inside a safe zone.

8.3 Escaping Into a Safe Zone +

Safe zones cannot be used to escape an active scene that began elsewhere.

Existing roleplay may continue into a safe zone where reasonable, but all parties must avoid disrupting unrelated players.

09
STAFF SUPPORT

Reports and Appeals

Use the correct process when reporting players, requesting support or appealing a punishment.

9.1 Complete the Roleplay First +

Unless immediate staff intervention is required, complete the active roleplay before submitting a report.

Do not break character or pause a scene simply because you believe another player has broken a rule.

9.2 Evidence +

Reports should include relevant video, screenshots, timestamps and player information where available.

Evidence must not be edited in a misleading way.

9.3 Honest Reports +

Reports and appeals must be truthful. Deliberately omitting important context, submitting false claims or attempting to manipulate staff may result in further action.

9.4 Appealing a Punishment +

Appeals should explain why the punishment was incorrect or why it should be reconsidered.

Repeated appeals without new information may be closed without further review.
